Location

Lądek Zdrój is located in the south-western part of the country, in the region called the Kłodzko Land, geographically belonging to the Sudetes. From an administrative point of view, the town belongs to the Lower Silesian Voivodship and Kłodzko County.

The village developed in the valley of the central White Lądecka River, which is the border separating the Golden Mountains (from the south and east) and the Śnieżnik Massif (from the north and west). The Biała Lądecka basin, which is the right-bank tributary of the Nysa Kłodzka River, covers 314.6 square kilometers. Since both Lądek and the above Silesian Stronie have sewage treatment plants, the river currents are inhabited by light stream trout.

The historical buildings of Lądek, not much disturbed today, make it possible to clearly distinguish the urban part with the preserved medieval urban layout and the spa part, distant from the city about 1.5 km.

The urban part is located in the place where the river forms a wide valley (about 420 m), while the streets and houses of the spa part occupy the bottom of the valley and climb the slopes of the surrounding hills (450-500 m). The climaxes of Zdrój are: Jerzy Hill, Kapliczne Hill and Maria Hill. Rich spruce and beech wood, beautiful meadows, numerous streams, create a beautiful, varied landscape around the village.

The climate of Lądek is shaped by its location among the mountains and their protection from damp and cold air masses. There is a mountain-bottom climate zone, the highest rated in terms of climatotherapeutic values, and the climate is described as mild, with wide adaptability for many people.
